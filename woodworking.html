I don't claim to know it all or to have all the best advice on the subjects of wood working, tool care and set-up. My aim is merely to pass on what I have learned over the years and what has worked for me.

My first experience with wood working was in my father's little boat building shop. It wasn't fancy, neither were his tools but he managed to turn out a number of small boats before he retired from that line of work. By that time I was only in primary school so my hands-on experience in the shop was very minimal but I none the less managed to remember a few things for later reference.

In later years I spent a good deal of my working life in construction and building restoration where I had to learn a variety of skills including that of wood working. For a period of time I also sold wood working tools which added to my base knowledge of stationary tools, hand and power tools. Here and there I have picked up tips from associates.

In earlier years I wish I had had access to some of the information I have included in this site as it would have advanced my ability and knowledge of tools, thus I have put together some of what I have learned in hopes it may help the novice or even someone experienced, with a different slant on processes.

As of this time I have not received tips from other wood workers to pass on but I hope as time passes to be able to include even more information from other folks.
